Writing Tips & Techniques

Show, Don't Tell

Instead of telling readers how characters feel, show it through their actions, dialogue, and body language.

Telling: "John was angry."

Showing: "John's fists clenched at his sides, his jaw tightening as he struggled to keep his voice steady."

Practice Exercise

Write a scene showing a character's emotion without directly stating what they're feeling.

Dialogue Mastery

Create natural, flowing dialogue that reveals character and moves the story forward.

Read dialogue aloud

Use contractions

Vary speech patterns

Scene Structure

Build compelling scenes with clear goals, conflict, and resolution.

  1. Set the scene
  2. Establish character goals
  3. Create conflict
  4. Build tension
  5. Resolve or escalate
